3D Javascript simulation of Olbers' Paradox, complete with interstellar extinction model due to non-luminous matter. Olbers' Paradox is intended to illustrate why the universe cannot be infinite with a uniform concentration of stars, but even to this day, textbooks leave out an important law of optics known as the Beer-Lambert Law, which states that light intensity decays exponentially with distance as it travels through light-absorbing material, such as a gas cloud, dust or an umbrella. Adjust the simulation parameters to visualize critical points at which the night sky would be completely bright, as opposed to the dark night sky we see. The crucial parameter "absorption" is the density of non-luminous matter distributed evenly throughout space. The more non-luminous matter there is in space, the more light will be absorbed from distant stars and the darker the night sky will appear. If the screen starts out all white, you might be too close to a star. Try decreasing the star radius or refresh the page to get a different random setup.
